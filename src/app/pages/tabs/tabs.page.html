Often times, I find that tabbing systems create a large DOM footprint and don't take advantage of Angular's latent capabilities to dynamically load/unload content. In addition, most tabbing systems have a very fixed layout, where a developer needs to play with a lot of customized CSS to bring about a desired appearance.

While there is additional room for configuration, these controls allow you to view how static content vs dynamic content can be generated, then controlled, outside or inside the tabs.
